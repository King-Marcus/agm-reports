Executive Summary

The short version: Better Dog has a stronger story than the market currently sees, but competitors are winning through review density, soft-chew formats, subscribe/coupon pressure, and condition-specific Amazon shelf ownership.

What competitors are doing differently

Cosequin and Dasuquin win joint support with massive review trust, multiple pack sizes, Best Seller / Overall Pick badges, high monthly unit velocity, and a clear vet-authority position. Zesty Paws wins allergy, calming, puppy, and multivitamin demand with a wide soft-chew portfolio, flavor/age variants, and frequent Subscribe & Save pressure. PetLab is using a gut-plus-allergy positioning that directly overlaps Better Dog's allergy and digestive products.

Better Dog is currently strongest where the market is also most contestable: Skin Magic and Allergy Balance. Those two products have enough ad efficiency to support controlled conquest, retargeting, and bundle testing.

What this means for Better Dog

Do not try to out-Cosequin Cosequin on generic joint terms first.
Use Forever Active for long-tail joint, senior mobility, and competitor PDP retargeting while building review/social proof.
Lead with itch/allergy and digestive remedies.
Skin Magic and Allergy Balance are Better Dog's best PPC proof points and map to the new subcategories.
Use Sponsored Display and AMC to convert warmer shoppers.
Better Dog should stop treating competitor traffic as broad prospecting only; retarget PDP viewers, storefront visitors, and category shoppers by condition cluster.

What Better Dog Sells That Competitors Do Not Own Cleanly

The best angle is not “another supplement”; it is Cesar-backed condition education plus liquid/formula differentiation.

Cesar Millan authority as a conversion asset

Competitors win trust with review count and vet framing. Better Dog’s differentiator is brand trust and behavior/whole-dog authority. That should show up in Sponsored Brands Video, Storefront condition pages, and PDP A+ content.

Condition bundles rather than isolated SKUs

Competitors often win one category at a time. Better Dog can package “itch + gut immune,” “digestive reset,” “calm routine,” and “senior active” journeys to lift AOV and explain why products work together.

Sponsored Display + AMC Market-Share Plan

This is the ad system to start taking share without blindly overbidding giant generic keywords.

Sponsored Display conquest

Build competitor ASIN groups by condition. Start with Skin Magic and Allergy Balance against Zesty/PetLab allergy/itch PDPs. Use Forever Active cautiously against Cosequin/Dasuquin long-tail PDPs. Keep Calm Confidence retargeting-first until conversion improves.

Approval-gated

Sponsored Display retargeting

Create 7/14/30-day viewed-but-not-purchased audiences by product cluster. Cross-sell Skin Magic viewers into Allergy Balance and Inner Purity; digestive viewers into Happy-Go-Lucky Belly; calm viewers into Calm Confidence/Calm Surrender only after PDP cleanup.

High fit

AMC audiences

Use AMC to isolate shoppers who viewed competitor ASINs and Better Dog ASINs, shoppers exposed to SP/SB/SD but not purchased, bought once but not Subscribe & Save, and storefront visitors by condition page.

Measurement layer
Key rule: Sponsored Display should not be judged only by same-day last-click ROAS. Use AMC to measure assisted conversions, view-through paths, repeat visits, NTB, Subscribe & Save conversion, and whether competitor-PDP exposure later converts through branded or organic terms.

Negative and cannibalization rules

Allergy Balance owns: allergy supplement for dogs, immune/allergy + digestive overlap.

Inner Purity owns: digestive cleanse / natural dog wormer / gut-cleanse territory after PDP review.
